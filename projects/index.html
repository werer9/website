ABOUT

REBECCA NEWMAN, a promising young Christchurch-based artist, graduated with a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree from the University of Canterbury in 2014. She commenced her studies in 2010 and later that year experienced the first of what became an unprecedented series of earthquakes that devastated Christchurch. She completed her degree in trying and difficult circumstances against the background of a devastated landscape, disruptions to everyday life and the necessity of compromise, sacrifice and loss. Having displayed a resilience and determination to complete her degree in these circumstances, it was natural her first major body of work, titled 'Light in the Redzone', reflected these challenging and trying times. Rebecca captured the images for this series in 2012 when she gained access to the central city cordon to document the devastation.

The 'Light in the Redzone' series of work is a limited edition of original digital photographs. While the hauntingly ethereal and powerful images vividly capture the absence of people, and the emptiness and devastation of post-quake central Christchurch, they also hint at the light of hope and progress as human intervention and recovery start to emerge within the scenes. We are reminded of the icons within the cordon - the traffic cones, the wire fences and the machinery. Intact buildings sit stoically among the ruins and partially deconstructed buildings silhouette the images, challenging us to remember the city scapes as they were before. Many of the buildings portrayed are now gone, giving this photographic documentary an important historical context.
